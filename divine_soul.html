Neem Karoli Baba or Neeb Karori Baba also known to followers as Maharaj-ji, was a Hindu guru, mystic and devotee of the Hindu deity Hanuman. He is known outside India for being the guru of a number of Americans who travelled to India in the 1960s and 1970s, the most well-known being the spiritual teachers Ram Dass and Bhagavan Das, and the musicians Krishna Das and Jai Uttal.Known as the “Miracle Baba” throughout north India, He manifested many siddhis (powers), such as being in two places at once or putting devotees in samadhi (state of God consciousness) at the touch of a finger. Maharaj-ji is best known for the unconditional love He showered on all who came into His presence as well as those who never met Him in the body but established a connection to him beyond the physical plane. He was a lifelong adept of bhakti yoga, and encouraged service to others (seva) as the highest form of unconditional devotion to God.

Neem Karoli Baba (Lakshmi Narayan Sharma) born in 1900 CE at Akbarpur, Uttar Pradesh, in a Brahmin family of Durga Prasad Sharma. It is said that in his 17th year, he was bestowed with the vision of Lord Hanuman and that is when Laksmi Narayan got enlightened about worldly happenings. When he was 11, Neem Karoli Baba was married to a Brahmin girl. Soon after his marriage, he left his home and wandered throughout the India as a sadhu and was known by many names. During his sadhna and tapasya at (Vavania) Morbi in Gujarat, he was known as Tallaiya Baba. Local inhabitants in Vrindavan would address him as Chamatkari Baba (miracle baba). Maharaj ji established at least 108 temples. His ashrams are in Kainchi, Vrindavan, Rishikesh, Shimla, Neem Karoli village near Khimasepur in Farrukhabad, Bhumiadhar, Hanuman Gadi, Lucknow, Delhi in India and in Taos, New Mexico, USA. Baba Neem Karoli would spend his winters in Vrindavan and summers in Kainchi Dham, Nainital. During his early ascetic day.

This is how Maharaj-Ji became known as Neem Karoli Baba; during his wandering, he boarded a train without a ticket; when TC (ticket-collector) approached, he explained his inability to pay the fine. As a result, the TC halted the train and kicked him off at Neeb Karori village, Nainital. After throwing him out, the TC found that the train wouldn’t start; even the driver couldn’t understand the reason. A few passengers suggested getting the ‘sadhu’ back on the train. On their advice the driver and the TC went inside the Neeb Karoli village, where they found the ‘Sadhu’ resting near the Kosi River.

Among the most well known of Neem Karoli Baba's disciples were spiritual teacher Ma Jaya, Ram Dass (the author of Be Here Now), teacher/performer Bhagavan Das, Lama Surya Das and the musicians Jai Uttal and Krishna Das. Other notable devotees include humanitarian Larry Brilliant and his wife Girija, Dada Mukerjee(former professor at Allahabad University, Uttar Pradesh, India), scholar and writer Yvette Rosser, filmmaker John Bush, and Daniel Goleman author of The Varieties of the Meditative Experience and Emotional Intelligence.
